Topic 1

Terms to Know for Parents- specifically of a child w/ a Learning Disability


I have basically determined the general idea of my webpage. Primarily, I want it to be a resource for parents of children who have a learning disability
in reading or a parent of a struggling reader, within the primary grade levels. I believe a crucial component to include would be familiarizing parents with some
of the special education verbage that they will hear or want to use when best addressing their child's needs. Below are just some of the key terms I would include:
  1. Accommodations: are supports and services provided to help a student access the general education curriculum and validly demonstrate learning.
    Some examples of accommodations: extended time on tests, assignments etc., setting of where they may take assessments and preferntial seating, level of support
    and if they need aide assistance, visual schedules, sensory items, sensory breaks, flexible seating or opportunities for movement.
  2. Inclusion: the belief that people with disabilities should have the same access to services, activities, and opportunities as everyone else.
    For example, people with disabilities learn in regular (not special-needs) classes whenever possible, if their needs can be met and work in places designed
    to accommodate their disability.
  3. Individualized Education Program (IEP):a written plan that outlines the educational goals and support needed for a student with disabilities.
    The IEP is agreed upon by the student's family, teachers, school administrators, and others who spend time with the student away from school.
  4. Modifications: individualized changes made to the content and performance expectations for students.
    Some examples of Modifications: quantity- modify the number of items that the child is expected to learn or complete, output: how a student responds to
    instruction (could be a different form of an assessment or verbally, on a whiteboard, Ipad etc.)

Topic 2

Strategies for Motivating A Struggling Learning- specifically during a non-prefered activty

  • Implent an incentive chart, board, bucket etc; I will provide some examples that myself and other teachers have used in the past within the link
  • Start small, begin with a warm up activity that you know they will succeed in
  • Provide immediate feedback with the incentive chart after simple tasks and gradually provide the feedback less quickly while working through the tasks
  • Consistently remind them what they are working towards
  • Use engaging materials; sometimes simply things like a whiteboard, highlighters, sight word games, fun manipulatives or pointers can make all the difference
